Hey Phil, does debt really have a silent b?

Yes it does, and it sounds crazy to a native speaker when someone tries to pronounce that b!

Debt entered the English language through Old French, and was spelled for many years as det.

Then, medieval scholars decided to add a b to make it look more like its Latin equivalent debitum, but people continued to pronounce it exactly as they had before.

To this day, debt rhymes with words like get, let and met.

Don't believe me? Listen to the pronunciation on the Cambridge Dictionary website.
