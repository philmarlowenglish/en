Why does debt have a silent b?

The word debt entered the English language via Old French, and was originally spelled "dette".

Medieval and renaissance scholars later felt that the English word should reflect its Latin origins more accurately, and decided to add a b to make it look more like its Latin equivalent debitum.

However, although the spelling changed in written texts, people countinued to pronounce it as they had before, true to its French origins.

The b isn't even a "soft b" — it's 100% silent, also in words like debtor and indebted.

So don't let the spelling confuse you: debt actually rhymes with many other English words like get, let, met, set and wet
